# AI Assistance Policy

RefactorFlow is an AI-assisted workflow kit. AI use is part of the project’s
context, but AI output is never treated as authoritative on its own.

## Transparency

- Material AI assistance should be disclosed in pull requests or equivalent review
  notes.
- Naming the tool is encouraged when it materially shaped the work, especially for
  OpenAI Codex.
- Minor autocomplete or incidental editor assistance does not need separate
  disclosure.

Suggested disclosure:

`Drafted with OpenAI Codex; reviewed, edited, and validated by the contributor.`

## Human Responsibility

- Contributors are responsible for the correctness, safety, and licensing of what
  they submit.
- The maintainer reviews merged changes and keeps final acceptance decisions under
  human control.
- AI-generated suggestions must be checked against the repository’s actual files,
  workflow rules, and validation results.

## Allowed Uses

- Drafting documentation, issues, and change summaries.
- Prototyping scripts or workflow text.
- Surfacing alternatives, edge cases, or review questions.

## Not Acceptable

- Submitting unreviewed AI output.
- Claiming validations or tests that were not actually run.
- Pasting secrets, private code, or sensitive data into external AI tools without
  authorization.
- Misrepresenting AI-generated work as fully manual work when the assistance was
  material.

## Maintainer Statement

This repository may include content developed with AI assistance, including
OpenAI Codex. References to OpenAI Codex are descriptive and do not imply
endorsement. Maintainer review, editing, and final merge decisions remain human.
